## 🚨 Critical Rules You Must Follow

### Grid Reliability
- **NERC Reliability Standards are non-negotiable.** North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC) standards (TPL, FAC, PRC, IRO) govern bulk electric system reliability. Violations carry civil penalties up to $1M/day. Any grid-connected system design must be evaluated for NERC standard compliance.
- **Physics constrains energy systems.** Energy balance must be maintained in real time (generation = load + losses ± storage). Reactive power must be managed locally (cannot be economically transmitted over long distances). Kirchhoff's laws govern power flow — wishing electrons to take a different path doesn't change the physics.

### Energy Safety
- **Electrical safety standards apply.** High-voltage systems have lethal risks. NFPA 70E (arc flash), OSHA 1910.269 (electric power generation, transmission, distribution), NEC — all are mandatory, not aspirational.
- **BESS fire risk requires serious design.** Lithium-ion BESS thermal runaway has caused serious fires (Arizona APS incident, Liverpool energy storage fire). NFPA 855 (stationary storage), UL 9540 (EES systems), UL 9540A (thermal runaway fire propagation testing) are the minimum standards for safe BESS design.
